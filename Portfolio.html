Digital Media Mix Model

Solution: build a media mix model to estimate the incremental impact of each ad channel. This led to three useful outcomes:

  • Validated that current levels of spend were delivering positive ROI (we were not over-spending)
  • Quantified the opportunity size for MMM-based spending: up to a 6 digit increase in incremental $USD profit per year
  • Testable hypotheses that we were under-invested in search ads and over-invested in social ads

A simplified version of the model*: \(Approved Credit Cards = 80 * \ln(Search Spend) + 20 * \ln(Social Spend) + 45 * \ln(DisplaySpend) + 130\)

Testing for Incremental Ad Impact

The Problem: We decided to test the hypothesis that we were under-invested in paid search advertising. We needed a way to formally test that increased spend would deliver incremental approved credit cards, as the model suggested.

The Solution: run a matched-markets test. This is a type of quasi-experiment, following this process:

  • Two geographical markets that historically follow similar performance trends are identified
  • Spending is increased (or, alternatively, can be paused) in only one of the two (the ‚Äútest market‚Äù)
  • All else being equal, performance changes in the test market that differ significantly from the control market can be attributed to the change in spend

Logistics Transformation

The process of booking cargo container space to move goods from point A to B has historically been fraught with challenges:

  • Pricing set out in complex, non-standardized contracts
  • Requiring back-and-forth communication with the carriers that own the ships
  • Subject to cancellation due to overbooking

Today, the major carriers are rolling out e-commerce style online booking tools. By connecting these tools with their inventory management and demand forecasting data, they can price container space more efficiently.

This presents opportunities to book at lower prices than the historical approach offers, with the guarantee that the purchase will not be cancelled due to overbooking. Naturally, demand for the lower priced container space is high, and space often sells out before companies can act.

The problem: My client, an international freight forwarder, wanted to find an automated way to secure affordable bookings using the e-commerce platforms on behalf of the manufacturers that they represent.
